### "The expectations of life depend upon diligence; the mechanic that would perfect his work must first sharpen his tools."

What tools you use help. Finding and getting to know tools and software can greatly help augment your productivity and achieve much more.

This post will differ to all my other posts on this site so far. I'd like to share and recommend to people the tools I use for my own productivity workflow. Do note though that this is not a one size fits all prescription but simply what I found to work for _ME_ over the past few years.

_Disclaimer: I am not being endorsed for any of the following software that appears in this post (I wish I was though...)_

### Trello
<img src="/assets/productivity/trello.png" alt="Drawing" align="middle" style="width: 200px;"/>

My favorite productivity app. This app allows you to create many good things like boards and lists. It's quite an effective to organise your thoughts.
- share with teams
- plan out projects
- see how things develop

### Wunderlist
<img src="/assets/productivity/wunderlist.png" alt="Drawing" align="middle" style="width: 200px;"/>
- differ to Trello
- short term goals and to do list whilst Trello is a longer term
- although not necessarily mutually exclusive

### Alfred
<img src="/assets/productivity/alfred.jpg" alt="Drawing" align="middle" style="width: 200px;"/>
- harder to explain but on Mac, hitting command + space bar, brings up a search tool that'll allow you to search for any tools/files on your laptop
- still need to discover it more
### Evernote
<img src="/assets/productivity/evernote.png" alt="Drawing" align="middle" style="width: 200px;"/>
- good note taking experience.
- cross devices so I type whatever comes to my mind on my phone which gets transferred on my laptop for viewing later
### Spark
<img src="/assets/productivity/spark.jpg" alt="Drawing" align="middle" style="width: 200px;"/>
- amazing.
- beautiful interface and effectively organises my emails for me
- fast and intuitive
- no mobile app :(
### Pocket
<img src="/assets/productivity/pocket.jpg" alt="Drawing" align="middle" style="width: 200px;"/>
- I read alot
- from all sorts of Mediums but never have enough time to read
- save for later and can also serve as a reading list
- doesnt save quite a few things like images etc
### Slack
<img src="/assets/productivity/slack.png" alt="Drawing" align="middle" style="width: 200px;"/>
- good for work related stuff
- differ to Messenger as less distractions and also see it as a space to do work
- hard to get people to use it
### Lastpass
<img src="/assets/productivity/lastpass.png" alt="Drawing" align="middle" style="width: 200px;"/>
- too many passwords so this is useful
- although kinda glitchy and logs me out of sites...
### Markdown + Atom
Yes I know I know. Markdown isn't a program. What I mean by this is that using Markdown to write things down compared to using Microsoft Word has saved me tremendous amount of time. No need to format and can just focus on writing. Atom text editor has a package I got that will just convert the markdown file into pdf. Perfect to then give out to people.

Now the following are just other things I use as well which do not necessarily carry over to my productivity.

### Audible
- Amazing hack to get onto your reading list
- Listen twice the speed to get through more since I strongly believe in Pareto principle applies to most books. 80% of the content and important bits comes from 20% of reading the book itself
- Wide variety
### Daylio
- nice little app to keep track of my mood
- lots of famous people keep a journal to reflect
- good statistical visualisation to see your sentiment throughout the month
### iTerm
- much better than terminal which comes standard on Macbook
### Google Drive
- good for syncing and backing up files
- i can now access stuff from phone as well!
- surprisingly quick to fill up 15GB free since gmail is included
